An archive of interactive projects by Visual Communication students at SUPSI for the Interaction Design course, exploring NASA's 70 years of space research, history, and discovery.

Constellation view

13 / 13

  1. 01

    Tags act as anchors. Every thematic tag is pinned to a fixed spot on the outer ring in alphabetical order. Because these positions are permanent, they create a reliable coordinate system for the entire map.

  2. 02

    Projects find their center of gravity. A project's position is calculated based on its tags. If a project is tagged with moon, apollo, and archive, it lands geometrically in the middle of those three anchors.

  3. 03

    Connections light up on hover. Relationships are revealed through interaction. Hovering over a project highlights all its tags on the outer ring, while hovering over a tag illuminates every project connected to it.

  4. 04

    Navigate the space. Scroll to zoom in and explore the constellation in detail. You can drag to pan across the view, and hover over any point to reveal the project's name and its connected tags. Tags are ordered alphabetically around the outer ring.
